A funding bill approved Wednesday by the House and Senate would require the departments of Veterans Affairs and Defense to develop a plan for an interoperable or single electronic health record system within six weeks, NextGov reports (Brewin, NextGov, 12/12).DOD and VA first launched an effort to create a joint integrated EHR, or iEHR system, in 2009.The iEHR project aimed to allow every service member to maintain a single EHR throughout his or her career and lifetime.However, DOD and VA officials in February announced plans to halt the iEHR project and instead focus on making their current EHR systems more interoperable.During a hearing of the House Armed Services and Veterans Affairs committees in July, officials from DOD and VA discussed their plans to develop a system to exchange standard clinical health data, rather than developing a joint EHR system as originally planned (iHealthBeat, 11/13).
