Privacy International defends the right to privacy across the world, and fights surveillance and other intrusions into private life by governments and corporations.Read more » Two new categories of surveillance systems were added into the dual-use goods and technologies control list of the Wassenaar Arrangement last week in Vienna, recognising for the first time the need to subject spying tools used by intelligence agencies and law enforcement to export controls.While there are many questions that still need to be answered, Privacy International cautiously welcomes these additions to the Wassenaar Arrangement.Undoubtedly, these new controls don’t cover everything they could, but the recognition that something needs to be done at Wassenaar level is a foundation to build from.Understanding what the new controls actually do is heavily based on how individual states implement the agreements.
