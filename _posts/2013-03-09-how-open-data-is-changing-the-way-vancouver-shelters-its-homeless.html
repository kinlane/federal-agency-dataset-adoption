Last month, seemingly out of nowhere, the Province of British Columbia announced it would spend $1 million (in Canadian dollars) to address health and safety violations in a number of the single resident occupancy (SRO) buildings it owns in Vancouver.The reason.Open data.Indeed, what follows is a wonderful example of how data and transparency can drive policy and encourage governments to fulfill commitments that help those most at risk.In January 2012, the Vancouver city council passed a motion mandating the creation of an online, searchable database of rental apartments in Vancouver, which would: utilize existing publicly available information collected by the City; and include information such as building owner, outstanding work orders, and property violations, if any.
