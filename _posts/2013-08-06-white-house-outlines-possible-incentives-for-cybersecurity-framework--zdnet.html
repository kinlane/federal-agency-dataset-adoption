The White House has published a new list of incentives designed to foster adoption for a national cybersecurity framework.See also: White House nominates venture capitalist as new FCC chairman|White House takes a stand on tech patent wars with several recommendations The framework follows up an Executive Order signed by President Obama earlier this year for increasing and improving the management of cyber risk.Thus, the list of incentives are being spun around three primary topics: information sharing, privacy, and adoption of cybersecurity practices.Some of the incentives being touted range from rather basic concepts such as federal grants and public recognition for adopting the framework to more specific offerings around risk-based insurance pricing and working with utility companies on rates and cybersecurity investments.
