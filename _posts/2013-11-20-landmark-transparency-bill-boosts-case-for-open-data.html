The Digital Accountability and Transparency Act (DATA) passed the United States House of Representatives this week by a 388-1 vote. The landmark transparency bill, which would standardize and publish federal spending data, is a major opportunity for the open data movement. It also paves the way for greater governmental effectiveness and efficiency, as well as increased technology innovation within the public sector.The landmark transparency bill, which would standardize and publish Federal spending data, is a major opportunity for the open data movement.DATA would require the Treasury Department to create government-wide data standards for agency financial reports, payments, budget actions, contract reporting, and grant reporting; direct agencies to use those data standards; and mandate that information be published online.
