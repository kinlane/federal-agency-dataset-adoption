In recent months, the number of government open data APIs has been increasing rapidly due to a variety of factors including the development of open data technology platforms, the launch of Project Open Data and a recent White House executive order regarding government data.ProgrammableWeb writer Mark Boyd has recently written three articles related to open data APIs; an article about the latest release of the CKAN API, an article about the UK Open Data Institute and an article about the CivOmega Open Data Search Engine.This post is a brief overview of several recent factors that have led to the rise of government open data APIs.According to Wikipedia the term “Open Data” has only recently been given a formalized definition: The above image shows US cities that have created an open data city government API or city services API(s).Some of the cities included in the map have created open data APIs, but have not yet created a government website providing access to their open data APIs and data.
