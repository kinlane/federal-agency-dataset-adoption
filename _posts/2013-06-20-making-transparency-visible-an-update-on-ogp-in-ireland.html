In my previous post on the Open Government Partnership website, I posed the question ‘Is Ireland closing the door on Open Government?’.At that time I expressed the view that Ireland’s government was uninterested in the benefits of open government.Now, eight months later, I am delighted to report that Ireland’s position on OGP has changed considerably.On May 20, 2013, our Minister for Public Expenditure and Reform, Mr Brendan Howlin, announced that Ireland has sent its Letter of Intent to join the Open Government Partnership.
